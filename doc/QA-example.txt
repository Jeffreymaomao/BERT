Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany. He is one of the most famous physicists of the 20th century, known for his theory of relativity, especially the special and general theories of relativity. In 1905, he published the famous equation E=mc², which describes the equivalence of energy and mass.

The Amazon rainforest is the largest tropical rainforest in the world, located in South America. It spans eight countries, with the majority of it located in Brazil. This rainforest is often referred to as the "lungs of the Earth" because it produces approximately 20% of the world's oxygen. The Amazon rainforest is also home to a rich diversity of wildlife, with many species of plants and animals found nowhere else on Earth.

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India, built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal is considered a jewel of Islamic art in India and was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983. It is renowned for its stunning architectural design and intricate decorations, attracting millions of visitors each year.
